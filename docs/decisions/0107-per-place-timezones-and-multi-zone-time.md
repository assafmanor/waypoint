## Context

A trip has a single `Trip.timezone` (default `UTC`, derived from the destination at creation, ADR-0032), and every event time is **rendered** against it (`formatTime(instant, trip.timezone)`), **authored** against it (`zonedIso(date, time, trip.timezone)`), and **day-framed** against it (`todayInTz`, the day-strip, day bucketing). This is wrong the moment a trip touches more than one zone — most sharply for transport that crosses zones: a flight departing Tel Aviv is stored as the correct instant but painted in the destination's (Tokyo's) time, so the traveler standing at the gate sees the wrong departure time.

The precise diagnosis (this ADR turns on it):

- **Storage is already zone-correct.** `startsAt`/`endsAt` are absolute UTC instants (ADR-0018); an instant needs no timezone. **No migration of existing instants.**
- **The now/next engine is already zone-correct.** `deriveNow`/`eventPhase`/countdowns compare instants against the clock, which is absolute. "What's now, when do we leave" works across zones today, untouched.
- **The bug is confined to three display/authoring roles**, all of which hardwire the one trip zone: rendering wall-clock labels, interpreting typed input, and day-framing ("today"/day-strip/bucketing).

The Maps & Places epic (ADR-0106) is about to give places real coordinates — from which an IANA zone is derivable — so the fix becomes buildable. That is why this rides that epic rather than standing alone.

## Decision (proposed)

**1. Places carry a timezone, derived and cached on the row.** A `Place` with coordinates resolves its IANA zone via a **free, offline lat/lng→zone lookup** (no Google Time Zone API cost, works offline), cached on the row like the rest of its Google enrichment (ADR-0048's "the `Place` row is the cache"). Resolved once at pick time. A name-only "Place-lite" (no coords) has no zone yet.

**2. Three roles of "timezone" that were conflated get separated** — this is the load-bearing move:

- **Authoring default** — the zone we interpret typed input in.
- **Event display zone** — the zone we render an already-created event in, forever after.
- **The live "now"** — the clock, the now-line, "today", the day-strip.

They must differ: if display simply followed the viewer's current context, a dinner planned at **19:00** would silently re-render as **20:00** the instant you cross a zone — a fixed plan appears to move. So **display is sticky; only the live "now" tracks your position.**

**3. An event's display/authoring zone resolves by a priority order:**

1. **Place attached** → the place's zone. **Transport is the one asymmetric case**: `startsAt` renders in the origin (`fromPlace`) zone, `endsAt` in the destination (`toPlace`) zone.
2. **No place** → the zone of the **itinerary segment** the event sits in. **Zone-crossing transport events partition the timeline into zone segments**: everything before the outbound crossing is the **origin/home zone** (known once the outbound flight's `fromPlace` is entered), everything after is the destination zone (and so on per crossing). A placeless event inherits its segment's zone.
3. **No anchoring transport at all** → the **trip primary zone** (see the "base" clarification below).

**"Base" is two different zones — name them apart.** They sit at opposite ends of the outbound flight and must not be conflated:

- **Origin/home zone** — the pre-outbound-crossing segment (Jerusalem), derived from the outbound flight's `fromPlace`; a _segment_ concept, not a stored trip field.
- **Trip primary zone** — the destination (Tokyo). This is what `Trip.timezone` demotes to (ADR-0032 already derives it from the destination): the Plan-mode framing anchor and the fallback when no transport is entered yet. It's the "actual trip timezone" a planner refers to before the trip.

They coincide only for a single-zone trip. One consequence to state, not discover: **before the outbound flight is entered, a pre-departure home event ("leave for airport 15:00") defaults to the trip primary (destination) zone** — the app doesn't know your origin until the flight exists — and **flips to the origin zone once the flight is added**. The editable zone chip (§6) covers the gap.

Worked example (the case that drove this): standing at Ben Gurion on flight day, "**coffee now**" is before the flight → **origin zone (Jerusalem)**; "**dinner tonight**," dropped after the flight in the day's order → **destination (Tokyo)** — correct even though the phone is physically in Israel.

**4. The live "now" tracks your position — via the itinerary, not GPS.** In Trip mode the clock/now-line/"today" sit in the zone of your **current itinerary segment** (which side of the nearest crossing you're on); in Plan mode they sit in the trip primary (destination) zone. Device GPS is **not** the driver (fragile at boundaries, permission-gated, and confidently wrong exactly when zones matter — standing at the origin airport, GPS says origin even when you mean the destination). Location, if ever used, is a confirmation nudge only.

**"Today" and the day-strip bounds roll at the _current segment's_ midnight** — a direct consequence: since the live "now" tracks your current segment, the calendar day rolls over at that zone's midnight, so "today" shifts on the travel day as you cross.

**5. `Trip.timezone` is demoted, not deleted.** It stops being every event's display authority and becomes the explicit **trip primary zone** (the destination, per ADR-0032): the fallback for placeless/pre-transport times and the Plan-mode framing anchor. It is _not_ the origin/home zone — that's a segment concept derived from the outbound flight (§3), never a stored trip field. (Renaming to `primaryTimezone` is optional cosmetic.)

**6. The resolved zone is always a visible, editable field.** The time input shows the inferred zone as a chip (e.g. "🕐 19:00 · Tokyo ▾"), one-tap correctable. Inference is never silently authoritative on the high-cost boundary cases — the design goal is "sensibly defaulted, trivially fixable," not a cleverer silent guess.

**7. Sticky display needs a home for a placeless event's zone.** Mechanism: a nullable **`Event.displayTimezone`**. _Store-vs-derive resolved (ADR-0110 §94-99):_ it is the **manual-override slot, not a cache of the resolved value** — null trusts the derivation (place > segment > trip primary), so adding the outbound flight reorients un-pinned placeless times; non-null is the user's chip-pinned zone, honoured forever. Its only writer is the zone chip (§6). The resolver reads `event.displayTimezone ?? eventDisplayZones(…)`. (Slice-1 field + resolver landed session 88; the chip writer is slice 4.)

**8. Edge-case rules:**

- **Attaching a place after authoring a placeless time** → **keep the wall-clock, shift the instant** (you meant the time _there_).
- **Which day(s) a zone-crosser files under → each end files under its own zone's calendar day**, via ADR-0064's per-day transition rows: a **departure transition entry** on the **origin day** in the **origin zone**, and an **arrival transition entry** on the **destination day** in the **destination zone**. So the day-strip reads "✈ depart 23:00 TLV" on day N and "✈ arrive 18:00 Tokyo" on day N+1. This reuses the existing transition-row mechanism rather than inventing a "which single day" rule, and it handles the date-line shift correctly (each end's day is computed in that end's zone).
- **Cross-zone times get a zone tag** so they aren't misread: "23:00 TLV → 18:00 +1 Tokyo".
- **No signal / mid-flight "local"** → mid-flight (between a crossing's departure and arrival) falls back to the **destination** zone (where you're heading); an unknown zone with no anchoring transport falls back to the **trip primary** zone.

## Consequences

- **No migration of existing instants** — they are absolute (ADR-0018). New: `Place.timezone` (derived/cached), a nullable `Event.displayTimezone` (pending §7), demoted `Trip.timezone` semantics; `@waypoint/shared` mirrors all of it.
- **The DST-correct machinery already exists and is already zone-parameterized.** `zonedIso`'s fixed-point wall-clock↔instant resolution takes a zone argument today (threaded with `trip.timezone`). The work is _threading the resolved per-event/segment zone through_ the display/authoring layer + a coords→zone resolver + the segment-partition helper — not inventing timezone handling. The `lib/time.ts` ponytail on DST-ambiguous hours stands unchanged.
- **The now/next engine is untouched** (instant-based). **Duration** (ADR-0084) stays zone-independent (instant-diff).
- **The place resolver extends, it doesn't multiply** — `lib/places.ts` (the linked/unlinked place resolver, ADR-0051) is where "which zone is this event in" naturally lives, plus a segment-partition helper keyed off transport. Reuse, per CLAUDE.md rule 8.
- **Transport events become load-bearing for zone inference** — a trip with no transport entered uses the base zone everywhere (fine, nothing crosses); adding the outbound flight reorients every placeless time after it to the destination. A satisfying "add the flight, the trip's clock reorients" behavior, but explicitly keyed to transport being entered.
- **Calendar sync (ADR-0003, deferred) lands more correctly** with per-place zones (it must already resolve place via booking/Place, ADR-0051).

## Where it sits in the Maps & Places phases (ADR-0106)

Not a seventh map phase — a **dependent workstream** riding the epic:

- **`Place.timezone` resolution folds into Phase 1** (the picker): when the picker lands coordinates, it also resolves + caches the zone. One small addition to that task.
- **The display/authoring/segment layer is a follow-on gated on Phase 1**, runnable **alongside Phases 2–3**, and **independent of the map rendering** (Phases 4/6) — it's a time-model change, not a map-surface one. The transport origin/destination split leans on the transport Place FKs already in the model (ADR-0048).

## Alternatives considered

- **Keep the single trip zone (status quo).** Rejected — wrong for transport and any multi-zone trip; the motivating bug.
- **Device-GPS-driven active zone.** Rejected — fragile at boundaries, permission-gated, and confidently wrong at exactly the moments zones matter (base airport on flight day). Itinerary structure is the reliable, offline, permission-free source.
- **Fluid display — re-render every event in the viewer's current zone.** Rejected — makes a fixed plan appear to move ("dinner slid to 20:00"); the reason display must be sticky.
- **Store a zone on every event.** Rejected as heavier than needed — a place drives the zone when present; only placeless events need to remember one (§7).
- **Store wall-clock + zone instead of a UTC instant.** Rejected — the instant is the correct absolute primitive (ADR-0018) and keeps now/next trivial; we add a display-zone hint, we don't replace the instant.
- **Bake the model into ADR-0106.** Rejected — it revises the core time primitive and the `Trip` schema, consequential enough to reason about on its own; it rides the epic but earns its own record.

## Amendment (2026-07-24, session 89) — slice 2: display wiring + the non-trivial-suppression rule

§6 said the resolved zone is "always a visible, editable field" — true of the **time input** (the editable chip, slice 4). But on **display** surfaces (the day timeline), rendering a zone label on every event would bury the signal: a single-zone Tokyo trip would repeat "· Tokyo" on every row. This amendment pins the display rule the ADR left implicit:

- **A zone label shows only when it is non-trivial** — i.e. when an end's resolved zone **differs from the day's ambient zone**. A **zone-crossing** event (start zone ≠ end zone, i.e. transport across zones) **always labels both ends**, since that contrast is the whole point (`23:00 · תל אביב → 18:00 +1 · טוקיו`). A single-zone trip therefore shows **no** labels anywhere — it renders exactly as before.
- **"Ambient zone of the day"** = the itinerary-**segment** zone spanning the day being viewed (`segmentZoneAt` at the day's noon), falling back to the trip primary zone. So on a settled destination day everything reads bare; a stray event in another zone (a pre-departure home event, a place in a foreign zone) gets a label; and the travel day labels whichever side isn't the day's ambient.
- **Mechanics (FE):** the pure decision lives in `lib/places.ts` — `eventZones(event, ctx) → { startZone, endZone, showStart, showEnd }` for a timeline range, and `eventEdgeZone(event, edge, ctx) → { zone, showLabel }` for a transition entry's single edge (ADR-0064). Both read `eventDisplayZones` (slice 1) + the day's `ambientZone`. `crossesMidnightZoned` (`lib/time.ts`) computes the "+1" across two zones. `EventCard`/`TransitionRow` gain optional zone props (absent → render wholly in the base `tz`, no label), so surfaces migrate incrementally with no regression.
- **Scope landed this slice: the Trip-mode day timeline** (`DayView` — `EventCard` rows + `TransitionRow` edges). The **Plan-mode builder** (`PlanDay`) and the **board hero** (`GlanceCard` now/next) still render in the single trip zone; the board's zone is the live-"now" context, so it lands with slice 3, and `PlanDay` follows. The label is **display-only** here — the editable chip that writes a `displayTimezone` override is slice 4.

> **Superseded by the session-90 amendment below.** In the shipped build the `· city` label read badly in the cramped timeline row — the IANA city is **English** (`Asia/Tokyo` → "Tokyo") inside an RTL Hebrew row, and two names + a range + `+1` wrapped. It was replaced by a **time-shift delta pill**; the suppression rule (show only when non-trivial) survives, now keyed on the shift being non-zero.

## Amendment (2026-07-24, session 90) — the zone display is a time-shift delta, not a city label; Plan mode wired

Design review of the shipped slice-2 row (a real trip: TLV → Reykjavik) showed the `· city` treatment was wrong on two counts: the label was English in a Hebrew RTL row, and it crowded the narrow time cell into an ugly wrap. The insight from that review: **what a traveler actually wants from a zone crossing is how far the clock jumps** — the time difference — not the zone's name. So the display becomes a **delta**:

- **The zone label is replaced by a signed time-shift pill** — amber (a time concept, design-language.md), e.g. `🕐 +6 ש׳` (Tokyo is 6h ahead) / `🕐 −3 ש׳` (Reykjavik 3h behind) / `🕐 +5:30` (fractional zones). It sits under the numeric time, so the time cell stays clean and the English city name is gone entirely (origin/destination identity already lives in the Hebrew route title).
- **The shift is what's shown, and the shift drives visibility.** For a **zone-crossing** event it's the destination clock minus the origin clock; for a **single-zone** event it's that zone minus the **day's ambient** zone. A **zero** shift shows nothing — so a single-zone trip stays bare, and two differently-named zones that happen to share an offset don't nag. (This subsumes the session-89 name-based suppression: the offset difference, not the zone id, is the signal.)
- **Mechanics (FE):** `eventZones(event, ctx) → { startZone, endZone, deltaMinutes? }` and `eventEdgeZone(event, edge, ctx) → { zone, deltaMinutes? }` (`lib/places.ts`); `zoneOffsetMinutes` + `formatZoneDelta` (`lib/time.ts`, the latter emits the signed `H ש׳` / `H:MM` token, minus sign never a hyphen). `EventCard`/`TransitionRow`/`PlanDay`'s `BuilderRow` render the pill under the time when `deltaMinutes` is set.
- **Plan mode is now wired too** (`PlanDay` — `BuilderRow` + its `TransitionRow`s), using the same per-day ambient (segment-at-noon). So both day surfaces read consistently; the **board hero** (`GlanceCard` now/next) remains for slice 3 (its zone is the live-"now" context).
- The **delta shown in previews and add/edit forms** — so a user is never confused about which zone a typed time is in — is the through-line into slice 4's authoring work, where each time field is entered _in its own endpoint's zone_ (departure origin, arrival destination) with the shift shown alongside.

## Amendment (2026-07-24, session 96) — slice 3: the live zone frames the day, but does not gate editing

§4 said the live "now" follows the **current itinerary segment's** zone, tracked through the itinerary rather than GPS. Wiring it exposed a case the ADR didn't cover: the live zone and a _specific day's_ zone are not the same question, and one call site was silently using the first to answer the second.

- **What the live zone frames (Trip mode):** "today", the day-strip anchor and its bounds, each day's scope (`past`/`today`/`future`), the now-line's clock, and the board. So the calendar day rolls at the midnight of the segment you are _in_ — fly TLV→Tokyo overnight and "today" advances on Tokyo's clock once you're past the departure instant, which is what a traveler on the ground means by the date. **Plan mode stays on the trip primary** — it is planning, not standing somewhere (§4).
- **What it must not gate: editing.** Under [ADR-0029](0029-trip-mode-day-scope-gating.md) a past day is a read-only archive, and `readOnly` had been a pure alias of `dayScope === 'past'`. Once the live zone advances mid-flight, that alias locks the day you are still flying through: crossing east, "today" becomes tomorrow while your travel day is still happening, and the day you're living turns into an archive. So the edit gate is answered in **the day's own ambient zone** (its segment zone at noon — the same ambient the delta pill already uses): `readOnly = todayInTz(dayAmbientZone, now) > date`. The scope _label_ still follows the live zone; only the gate is local. **A day ends when that day's clock says so.**
- **Mechanics (FE):** `currentZone(nowMs, crossings, primaryZone)` (`lib/places.ts`) — `segmentZoneAt` with the primary as fallback, still pure and clock-free (the caller passes the instant). The trip's crossings are derived **once** in `trip-state` (`zoneCrossings` on the context) rather than per surface, so the per-minute clock tick re-reads them instead of re-deriving them from every event, booking and place.
- **Still open from this slice:** the board hero's **per-event** zones + shift pill. `GlanceCard` takes a single `tz`, so rendering each now/next event in its own zone is a further change; the hero's _framing_ zone is correct as of this slice.

## Amendment (2026-07-24, session 99) — a booking's zone override is per-end, and lives on the booking

§7 named one mechanism for the sticky-display zone: a nullable `Event.displayTimezone`. Slice 4b shipped the chip on it and found the limit — that field is **one zone for the whole event**, and `eventDisplayZones` collapses both ends onto it. A zone-crossing booking is precisely the case that can't be expressed that way: pinning a flight's departure would erase its arrival's zone, and with it the crossing that partitions the itinerary.

- **Two nullable columns on `Booking`** — `startDisplayTimezone`, `endDisplayTimezone` — following the **same authority rule as its place columns** (ADR-0048): for transport `start` is the origin's and `end` the destination's; for a single-place booking only `start` is used, and it drives both ends. `Event.displayTimezone` keeps its documented job: the placeless standalone event.
- **Priority becomes per-end**: event override > booking's per-end override > endpoint place > itinerary segment > trip primary. So a flight can take its origin from a pinned zone and its destination from a real place.
- **Why an override is needed at all when §3 says the place decides:** a place answers only if it has coordinates. A **coordless Place-lite** — minted when you're offline or when Google matched nothing (ADR-0108) — has no zone, so before this the form silently fell back to the trip primary with no way to correct it. That is the whole scope of the override: the chip is **editable only when no place answers**, and a placed endpoint keeps a read-only statement, because there the honest edit is the place itself.
- **One resolution point:** `bookingEndZones(booking, places)` returns each end's zone _or undefined_ (never a fallback), and both `tripZoneCrossings` and `eventDisplayZones` read it. That is what makes a pinned pair a real crossing: pin an origin and a destination on a flight between two coordless places, and the itinerary partitions on it exactly as it would for two real airports.

## Amendment (2026-07-24, session 100) — a day's zone is evidenced by that day, and "now" by the plan around now

Reported from a real trip: with one outbound TLV→KEF flight in the itinerary, the now-line read **21:31** while the phone said **00:31**, and two bookings on a later day — one in Nicosia, one in Israel, the **same offset as each other** — each showed a `+3` shift pill. Both symptoms trace to one rule: sessions 89-90 defined the day's ambient zone (and session 96 the live zone) as the **itinerary segment**, i.e. the destination of the last crossing. After a single outbound flight that is the destination zone for the rest of the trip, so:

- a day whose own bookings are all in Cyprus was framed in Iceland, and
- the shift pill — which is supposed to mean "this event is somewhere other than the rest of this day" — measured every event against a zone **nothing on that day was in**, giving two same-offset events a pill each.

The segment is a fine answer when nothing better is known. It is the wrong answer when the day itself says otherwise:

- **A day's ambient zone** is the zone its own events agree on, when the ones with a _known_ zone (a pin or a place with coordinates) share a **UTC offset** at that day's noon; else the segment at noon; else the trip primary. Agreement is by offset, not zone id, so Nicosia and Jerusalem are one ambient rather than a mixed day. Only _known_ zones vote — a placeless event's zone **is** the segment zone, so letting it vote would confirm the segment and make the rule a no-op — and a zone-**crossing** event never votes, since it is the thing that moves you between zones. A multi-day stay votes on its middle nights (where you are sleeping).
- **The live "now"** (Trip mode's clock, now-line, "today", the day-strip anchor) is where the plan says you are _right now_: an event **in progress** (a crossing in progress reads its destination, §8), else the **nearest** known-zone event within `LIVE_ZONE_WINDOW_MS` (half a day either side — an hour-away booking places you, one five days out doesn't), else the ambient zone of the day the segment puts you in.
- Everything stays **itinerary-driven, never GPS** (§4). This is a better reading of the same evidence, not a new signal.
- A **genuine travel day** is unaffected: its events span two zones, so the consensus abstains and the segment answers, exactly as before — mid-flight still reads the destination.

The ADR-0029 read-only gate rides the same corrected ambient, so a day now ends when the clock of the place that day was actually spent in says it does.
